Analysis on the Relationship between Prison Incarcerations, Admissions, Releases and Race¶

By Felix Gu

Introduction¶

In the landscape of criminal justice, few issues are as deeply intertwined and contentious as those surrounding race and prison incarcerations. The United States has the highest incarceration rate of any country in the world and the intersection of race and incarceration presents a complex tapestry of social, economic, and systemic factors that have profound implications for individuals, communities, and society as a whole.

Incarcerations reflect broader societal issues such as inequality, discrimination, and systemic biases within the criminal justice system. Addressing disparities in incarcerations is essential for promoting social justice and ensuring equal treatment under the law for all individuals, regardless of race, ethnicity, or socioeconomic status. Incarcerations impact not only individuals but also families, communities, and society as a whole. High rates of incarceration can destabilize communities, disrupt families, and perpetuate cycles of poverty and crime. Addressing mass incarceration is essential for promoting community well-being and fostering safer, healthier neighborhoods.

This project examines the US incarceration rate, along with the admission and release rates of documented races within the state of Arizona from 2015-2022. This smaller scale view is not a reflection of the issue within America, it's not even a reflection of the issue within the state of Arizona. But a smaller data set is easier to examine for the purposes of a tutorial. Here, we will walk through the steps of the Data Science Lifecycle, as well as the code and analysis accompanying it.

def plot_scatter_with_regression(ax, x, y, title):
    ax.scatter(x, y, alpha=0.5)
    m, b = np.polyfit(x, y, 1)
    ax.plot(x, m*x + b, color='red')
    ax.set_title(title)
    ax.set_xlabel('Date (numeric)')
    ax.set_ylabel('Incarceration Count (total)')

It measures the strength of the relationship between the dependent and independent variables by measuring the amount of variance in the dependent variable captured by the model. This means there is a strong correlation between race, and total incarcerations.

Insight and Policy Decision¶

We're at the end of our journey! Here we take all of our analysis and observations from the previous steps and draw our conclusions from them! There a a couple things to note from our journey down.

  1. There is an overall negative trajectory towards incarcerations over all races.
  2. Incarcerations, admissions, and releases are all dominated by White and Hispanic people overall.
  3. Our linear regression model demonstrates a strong correlation between race and total incarcerations.

This topic however is one that definitely needs more work. The issue of incarceration and race are both fundamentally American issues that are worthy of intense and dedicated study.
